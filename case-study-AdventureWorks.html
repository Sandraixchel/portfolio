Featured case study

Adventure Works

This group project analyzed the Adventure Works 2019 dataset to identify key drivers of business performance across sales, human resources, and store operations. Using SQL for data extraction and Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, NumPy) for exploratory data analysis and visualization, the team investigated relationships between HR metrics, store characteristics, and revenue generation across multiple geographic markets.

  • Python
  • SQL
  • Matplotlib
  • NumPy
  • Pandas
Timeline: October · 2025

Power Point Presentation

Delivered to our cohort and mentor, showing a clear narrative flow connecting data insights to business implications.

Key Highlights

  • USA dominates in absolute revenue ($25M+), but Canada leads in sales per inhabitant demonstrating strong market penetration and per-capita consumer engagement
  • Sales representatives take significantly less sick leave than office-based workers, indicating better work-life balance and flexibility in field-based roles
  • Departments with manual labour tend to take more sick leave revealing potential burnout or ergonomic issues in physically demanding roles
  • Larger stores correlate with more employees (as expected), but store size itself is NOT a key revenue driver, challenging conventional retail assumptions
  • Scatter plot analysis reveals weak correlation between employee numbers and revenue, indicating that strategic staffing and location matter more than store size for profitability

Methodology

Data Extraction & Preparation

  • Used SQL queries to extract relevant tables from Adventure Works 2019 (sales, employees, stores)
  • Joined multiple data sources to create analysis-ready datasets with HR, operational, and financial metrics

Exploratory Data Analysis

  • Employed pandas for data cleaning, filtering, and aggregation across multiple dimensions
  • Calculated correlation matrices and descriptive statistics to identify relationships between variables

Visualization

  • Created matplotlib visualizations (scatter plots, bar charts, line charts) to illustrate key findings
  • Presented our findings to peers and mentor, effectively communicating the business implications of our data analysis through a structured narrative and meaningful visualizations
